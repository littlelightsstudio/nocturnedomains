The Storyworld

The nocturnal world is not a parallel dimension.
It is a hidden layer of our own.

Nocturnal creatures live in shadows and dark places, invisible to ordinary humans. Goblins have built entire cities beneath the streets of the world's great metropolises, running on Nocturnal Energy: a natural force they have learned to harness, engineer, and refine over centuries.

Dreams are not random. They are engineered and delivered by goblin field units every night, crafted from templates and filled with each dreamer's own memories and emotions. Children dream more vividly than adults, and the best goblin engineers compete to work with them.

Helena Flinn is twelve years old, chronically sleep-deprived, and the only human alive who can sense and direct Nocturnal Energy. Which makes her, depending on who you ask, either the most remarkable person in two worlds or the biggest problem in both.

The Trilogy

Book One

Helena Flinn and the Secrets of the Night

The Helena Flinn Chronicles

Helena has suffered from sleepless nights and terrible nightmares as long as she can remember. When she joins a new sleep therapy group in Vienna, she doesn't expect much. Then a desperate goblin arrives at her window with a plea for help — and leads her into a hidden steampunk city beneath the streets she grew up on. Something ancient is breaking through the dream system. Helena, it turns out, is the only one who can stop it.

Dream Science

Six Known Types of Dream

As classified by the Vinaborg Dreamcraft Manual, 4th Edition. Two further entries remain restricted.

Normal Dream

Engineered and delivered; the dreamer's subconscious fills the variables. The baseline. Millions produced every night.

Intense Dream

Specially crafted for highly receptive individuals. Requires a senior engineer. Not assigned lightly.

Normal Nightmare

The brain injects difficult variables to process unresolved issues. Not caused by goblins. Usually helpful, in the long run.

Recurring Nightmare

An unresolved issue, on repeat. Treatable via the Mastery Dream technique: invent an alternative ending and rehearse it before sleep.

Daydream

Possibly an unfinished engineered dream spilling into waking hours. Helena is a vivid daydreamer. Whether this is a cause or a symptom remains under investigation.

Lucid Dream

Consciously controlled by the dreamer. Rare. Trainable, to a degree. A building block for something far more significant.

???

The Vinaborg Dreamcraft Manual lists a seventh type. The entry is sealed. Access restricted to senior bureau members only.

???

There are those who claim to have experienced something that fits none of the known categories. The bureau does not comment on this.

The Author

My name is Michael, though most people call me miso. I live just outside Vienna with my wife and two daughters. In my day job, I am the CEO and Creative Director of Little Lights Studio — a Vienna-based film and brand agency with eleven years of experience. I have a long history of telling stories for other people. The Helena Flinn Chronicles is where I tell one for myself.

I have been building the world of Nocturne Domains for years. The goblins came first, then the dream engineering system, then Helena. The city beneath the city felt inevitable: Vienna has so much history underground already. I just gave it a different kind of inhabitant.

The Helena Flinn Chronicles began as a story about a girl who doesn't sleep well and discovers she is extraordinary because of it, not in spite of it. It became something larger: a trilogy about the relationship between imagination and science, between what we can measure and what we can only feel, between belonging to one world and finding yourself responsible for two.

Both books are available now. The Song of the Ancients is in development for 2026. The story is not finished.
